THE USER

This game is thoughtfully designed for children aged 5-6 with learning disabilities, focusing on accessibility, engagement, and essential skill-building. It supports young learners eager to master foundational concepts at their own pace, parents seeking adaptable tools to complement their child’s unique learning style, and teachers dedicated to creating inclusive and effective strategies for their students. By combining fun, flexibility, and accessibility, the game empowers its users to overcome challenges and thrive in their learning journeys.

THE EDUCATION SYSTEM

Many schools offer additional services for special needs students, such as speech-language therapy and occupational therapy. These are often based on alternative learning expectations that are specified by an Independent Education Plan (IEP). 1 in 5 teachers feel untrained in helping and teaching kids with learning disabilities. Due to the lack of funding many teachers get a minimal amount of training in helping kids with learning disabilities. Through interviews, they found that 25% of teachers believe that ADHD and add are caused by bad parenting and do not count as a learning disability.

Six Strategies to Support Learning:

1. Clear Instructions– Simplify and use storytelling.
2. Hands-On Learning– Use textures, colors, and physical tools.
3. Visual Aids– Include big text and visual elements in tasks.
4. lexible Timing– Allow extra time and break down tasks.
5. Interactive Tools– Encourage typing, writing, and task-specific tools.
6. Audio and Reading– Offer audiobooks or reading options.
